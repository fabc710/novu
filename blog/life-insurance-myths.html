7 Life Insurance Myths
That Are Costing You
Peace of Mind

Sofia Mendez Licensed Life Insurance Specialist
March 10, 2025

Few financial topics are surrounded by as much misinformation as life insurance. Many people put off getting coverage for years — not because they don't care about their families, but because they believe something about life insurance that simply isn't true.

These misconceptions can be costly. They leave families exposed, lead people to overpay, or cause them to skip protection entirely. Below, our licensed agents clear the record on the seven most common myths we hear from New York clients — so you can make a confident, informed decision.

Key Takeaways
  • Term life insurance is far cheaper than most people assume
  • Buying young locks in lower rates for the life of the policy
  • Employer coverage is rarely enough — and disappears if you change jobs
  • Stay-at-home parents provide real, insurable economic value
  • Death benefits are generally income-tax-free to your beneficiaries
01

"Life Insurance Is Too Expensive"

This is the number one reason people delay coverage — and it's almost always based on a guess, not a quote. Studies consistently show that most consumers overestimate the cost of life insurance by three times or more.

A healthy person in their 30s can often secure a substantial term life policy for roughly the price of a streaming subscription or two per month. Term life — which covers you for a set period like 10, 20, or 30 years — is designed to be affordable and straightforward.

The Reality: You won't know your real price until you get a quote. It's almost always lower than you expect — and a Novu agent can compare carriers for you in minutes.
02

"I'm Young and Healthy — I Don't Need It Yet"

It feels logical to wait, but it usually works against you. Life insurance is priced primarily on age and health, and both only move in one direction over time. The younger and healthier you are when you buy, the lower your rate — and that rate is typically locked in for the entire term.

Waiting also carries a hidden risk: a new diagnosis or health change can make coverage more expensive, or harder to qualify for, later. Buying earlier is one of the few financial decisions that gets more valuable the sooner you act.

Important: The best rate you'll likely ever see is the one available to you today. Locking it in young protects your future insurability.
03

"My Employer's Coverage Is Enough"

Group life insurance through work is a nice benefit — but it's rarely sufficient on its own. Employer policies typically cover only one to two times your annual salary, while experts often recommend coverage of 10x income or more to truly protect a family.

There's a second catch: employer coverage is usually not portable. If you change jobs, get laid off, or retire, that protection generally ends — often at the very moment you may be hardest to insure elsewhere. A personal policy stays with you no matter where you work.

The Reality: Treat employer coverage as a bonus layer — not your foundation. A personal policy ensures your family is protected regardless of your job status.
04

"Stay-at-Home Parents Don't Need Life Insurance"

A stay-at-home parent may not earn a paycheck, but the work they do has very real economic value. Childcare, transportation, meal preparation, household management, and more would cost a significant amount to replace if that parent were no longer there.

Life insurance on a stay-at-home parent helps the surviving family cover those costs and maintain stability during an incredibly difficult time — without taking on debt or making drastic lifestyle changes.

05

"I Have a Will — So My Family Is Covered"

A will and a life insurance policy do two very different jobs. A will directs how your existing assets are distributed — but it doesn't create new money for your family, and it often must pass through probate, which can take months.

Life insurance, by contrast, pays a death benefit directly to your named beneficiaries, usually quickly and outside of probate. It's designed to put cash in your family's hands exactly when they need it — for the mortgage, bills, and day-to-day living.

06

"My Family Will Be Taxed on the Payout"

This worry stops many people from buying enough coverage — but in most cases, a life insurance death benefit is paid out income-tax-free to your beneficiaries. The full amount you choose is generally the amount your family receives.

There are nuances in specific situations (for example, very large estates), which is exactly why it's worth talking to a professional. But for the typical family, the payout is designed to arrive intact, when it matters most.

Note: Tax rules vary by situation. Novu can help you structure coverage correctly and coordinate with your tax advisor when needed.
07

"The Application Process Is Long and Invasive"

It used to be that every policy required a lengthy application and a medical exam. Today, many carriers offer simplified-issue and no-medical-exam options that can be approved in days — sometimes the same day — for qualifying applicants.

And you don't have to navigate it alone. As an independent agency, Novu handles the comparison, paperwork, and carrier communication for you, so getting protected is far simpler than most people expect.

The Bottom Line

Life insurance isn't as expensive, complicated, or unnecessary as the myths suggest. For most families, it's one of the most affordable and meaningful ways to protect the people they love — and the cost of waiting is almost always higher than the cost of coverage.
